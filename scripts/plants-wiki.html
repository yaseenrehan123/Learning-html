Apple

The apple plant (Malus domestica) is a deciduous tree in the rose family, known for its sweet, crisp fruit. Native to Central Asia, it has been cultivated worldwide for centuries. Apple trees thrive in temperate climates and produce fragrant blossoms that give way to a wide variety of apples, prized for their taste and nutritional value.

Mango

The mango plant (Mangifera indica) is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the cashew family. Native to South Asia, it is widely cultivated for its juicy, flavorful fruit. Mango trees thrive in warm climates and are known for their dense canopies, fragrant blossoms, and the iconic sweet and tangy mangoes they produce.

Potato

The potato plant (Solanum tuberosum) is a root vegetable that belongs to the nightshade family. It is native to the Andes in South America and is widely cultivated for its edible tubers, which are a staple food worldwide. The plant grows best in cool climates and produces small, star-shaped flowers and green leaves.

Orange

The orange plant (Citrus sinensis) is a citrus tree known for its sweet, tangy fruit. Native to Southeast Asia, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. Orange trees are evergreen, featuring glossy green leaves and fragrant white blossoms that develop into the bright, juicy oranges enjoyed worldwide.

PineApple

The pineapple plant (Ananas comosus) is a tropical herbaceous plant known for its sweet, tangy fruit. Native to South America, it is widely cultivated in warm climates. Pineapple plants have spiky, strap-like leaves and produce a single large fruit from the center, which is valued for its flavor and nutritional benefits.

Pomegranate

The pomegranate plant (Punica granatum) is a deciduous shrub or small tree known for its nutrient-rich, jewel-like fruit. Native to the Middle East and South Asia, it thrives in warm, dry climates. The plant produces vibrant red flowers and round fruits filled with juicy, seed-filled arils prized for their sweet-tart flavor and health benefits.
