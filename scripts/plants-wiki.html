Apple

The apple plant (Malus domestica) is a deciduous tree in the rose family, known for its sweet, crisp fruit. Native to Central Asia, it has been cultivated worldwide for centuries. Apple trees thrive in temperate climates and produce fragrant blossoms that give way to a wide variety of apples, prized for their taste and nutritional value.

Potato

The potato plant (Solanum tuberosum) is a root vegetable that belongs to the nightshade family. It is native to the Andes in South America and is widely cultivated for its edible tubers, which are a staple food worldwide. The plant grows best in cool climates and produces small, star-shaped flowers and green leaves.

Mango

The mango plant (Mangifera indica) is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the cashew family. Native to South Asia, it is widely cultivated for its juicy, flavorful fruit. Mango trees thrive in warm climates and are known for their dense canopies, fragrant blossoms, and the iconic sweet and tangy mangoes they produce.
